FAQ ANSWERING TASKS
====================

1. Quick Answer
---------------
Give a clear and simple reply to the question. Add a short reason or extra note only if it helps.

2. Step-by-Step
---------------
For how‑to questions, start with a short overview, then give steps in order. Add tips or warnings if needed, and what to try if something doesn’t work.

3. Different Situations
-----------------------
If the answer changes based on the person or case, explain each scenario separately and why it matters.

4. Simplifying Tech
-------------------
If the question is technical, explain it in everyday words. Use examples or comparisons if it helps. Focus on what the user needs to know, not the deep details.

5. Troubleshooting
------------------
For problem questions:
- Say the direct answer first
- List common causes
- Suggest fixes to try
- Note when to get help from support
- Share tips to avoid the problem later

6. Comparing Options
--------------------
If the question asks about two or more choices:
- Describe each one
- Highlight main differences
- Give pros and cons
- Suggest when each might be best
- Use a small table if that makes it clearer

7. Policy or Procedure
----------------------
When it’s about a rule or process:
- State the policy simply
- Say why it exists
- Who it applies to and any exceptions
- Where to look for details
- Who to contact for unusual cases

8. Product or Service
---------------------
If the FAQ is about a product or service, give the direct answer, how it works, limits or requirements, and mention related features if useful.

9. Answer with Examples
-----------------------
Reply to the question and add a couple of examples to show what you mean. Explain why they fit and how someone could use them.

10. When Escalation is Needed
-----------------------------
For tough questions:
- Answer what you can directly
- Point out what needs more help
- Say who to contact
- Mention what info they’ll need
- Share how long it might take to fix
- Suggest temporary workarounds

11. Prerequisites
-----------------
If the question needs certain things ready first, list those, then explain the answer step by step. If not ready, explain alternatives.

12. Summary Style
-----------------
For big or common questions, structure the answer as:
- A short reply for quick readers
- A detailed explanation for those who want more
- Common follow‑ups with answers
- Related ideas to check out
- Best practices or advice
